South African Government to Crack Down on Online Financial Fraud

Press Release · Partnering with international firms, MOU to be signed

Enoch Godongwana, South Africa’s Finance Minister

Published: 25 November 2025 · By News24

The South African Government today announced its commitment to intensify efforts against online financial fraud, partnering with major international financial oversight and cybersecurity organisations. A Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) is to be signed, enabling a coordinated, cross-border response to digital scams and fraudulent investment schemes.

Growing Threat of Fraud

Authorities have highlighted a worrying increase in fraud cases that exploit emerging technologies. Deepfake videos, cloned websites, and cross-border scam operators are among the tactics being used to deceive unsuspecting investors.

Minister Godongwana Leads the Push

Finance Minister Enoch Godongwana is at the helm of this initiative. His office has reportedly held extensive discussions with global regulatory entities and private security firms to define the mechanism and scope of the anticipated collaboration.

Key Objectives of the MOU

  • Establish a framework for sharing intelligence on fraudulent networks.
  • Deploy advanced detection tools to identify and dismantle scam platforms.
  • Coordinate rapid cross-border takedowns of fraudulent sites.
  • Run public awareness campaigns to educate citizens about risks.

Expected Outcomes

Once fully implemented, this partnership could significantly improve South Africa’s resilience against online scams. Officials believe the initiative will help reduce fraud losses and restore trust in digital financial services.

Further details — including the names of partner firms and the formal signing schedule — will be disclosed after the MOU is formalised.
