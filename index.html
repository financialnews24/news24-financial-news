South African Government to Crack Down on Online Financial Fraud Through New International Partnership
Treasury moves to strengthen digital financial security as global cybercrime syndicates target South African citizens.
Pretoria – 26 November 2025

The South African government is set to introduce one of its most comprehensive measures yet to combat online financial scams, fraudulent investment schemes, and cross-border digital crime. Finance Minister Enoch Godongwana announced that the National Treasury will formalize a multi-country partnership aimed at improving detection, prevention, and response to internet-based financial fraud.

According to Treasury officials, an extensive Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) is scheduled to be signed in the coming weeks. The agreement includes collaboration with global cybersecurity firms, international financial-crime task teams, and digital-forensics specialists.

“Over the past three years, South Africans have increasingly become targets of international online fraud networks. These schemes operate across jurisdictions, making enforcement difficult. This partnership marks a major step forward in protecting citizens and strengthening our regulatory capacity,” said Minister Godongwana.

The initiative will introduce advanced fraud-monitoring systems, real-time data-sharing channels, and enhanced verification frameworks for digital financial platforms operating within South Africa. Officials also confirmed that public-facing awareness campaigns will accompany the rollout, ensuring consumers can better identify deceptive online schemes.

Treasury emphasized that the project aligns with South Africa’s broader financial-sector modernization plan, which prioritizes digital safety, economic integrity, and improved cross-border cooperation. Further updates will be released once the MoU has been formally executed.
